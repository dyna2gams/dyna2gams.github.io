An optimal control problem

rem: An optimal control problem

Consider the optimal control problem:

\[ \min_{u} \quad x(1) - \alpha \int_0^1 x^2(t) \ \mathrm{d}t\\ \dot{x}(t) = u(t)\\ x(0)=0 \]

The control \(u\) takes values in \([-1,1]\) and \(\alpha \in \mathbb{R}\). We want to find all locally optimal controls.

If \(\alpha < 0\), the optimal solution is \[ \begin{cases} x(t)=0 , ~~ & u(t)=0, ~~ & \lambda(t)=0 & \text{ for }t \leq t_1, \text{ and }\\ x(t)=t_1-t , ~~ & u(t)=-1, ~~ & \lambda(t)= -\alpha(t-t_1)^2 & \text{ for }t > t_1. \end{cases} \] with \(t_1=1-\sqrt{\frac{1}{|\alpha|}}\).

For \(\alpha \geq 0\), \[ x(t)=-t, \qquad u(t)=-1, \qquad \lambda(t)=1+\alpha(1-t^2) \] this time without further restrictions on \(t\). This is the optimum within the boundaries of the control.
