Robust PID Design

rem: Robust PID Design

The objective of the proposed PID design method is to find the PID controller giving the least integrated absolute error (IAE) value when a load disturbance \(d\), modelled as a step, is acting on the closed loop system. The optimization is done under the constraints that the closed loop system is stable and that the open loop Nyquist curve is tangent to one or two prespecified circles in the complex plane without entering either of them, thus maximizing the gain. These two circles are called the \(M_s\)- and \(M_t\)-circles, which sizes and positions are given by \[ M_s = \max_{\substack w} |S(i w)| \\ M_t = \max_{\substack w} |T(i w)| \] where the sensitivity function and the complementarity sensitivity function are defined as \[ S(s) = \dfrac{1}{1+L(s)}, \; T(s) = \dfrac{L(s)}{1+L(s)} \] and \(L(s) = C(s) P(s)\) is the loop transfer function.

The resulting, non-convex, optimization problem can be written as \[ \min_{\substack K,T_i,T_d \in \mathbb{R}^+} \int_0^{\infty} |e(t)| \ \mathrm{d}t = IAE_{dist} \] subject to \[ r_s - |L(i \omega) - c_s| \leq 0 \;\; \forall \ \omega \in \mathbb{R}^+ \\ r_t - |L(i \omega) - c_t| \leq 0 \;\; \forall \ \omega \in \mathbb{R}^+ \] with \[ c_s = -1, \; r_s = \dfrac{1}{M_s} \\ c_t = \dfrac{-M_t^2}{M_t^2-1}, \; r_t = \dfrac{M_t}{M_t^2-1} \]

The robustness constraints can be generalized to settings with process uncertainties. Consider a process \(\tilde{P}\) with uncertainties such that for each frequency point the Nyquist plot is known to lie within a circle with a frequency dependent radius \(\rho\), i.e., \[ \tilde{P}(i \omega) = P(i \omega) + \Delta P(i \omega), \; |\Delta P(i \omega))| \leq \rho(\omega) \] Hence, the robustness constraints can be formulated as \[ r_s - |L(i \omega) - c_s| + \rho(\omega) |C(i \omega)| \leq 0 \;\; \forall \ \omega \in \mathbb{R}^+ \\ r_t - |L(i \omega) - c_t| + \rho(\omega) |C(i \omega)| \leq 0 \;\; \forall \ \omega \in \mathbb{R}^+ \]

In this example, the transfer functions are \[ P(s) = \dfrac{9}{(s+1)(s^2+s+9)} e^{-s} \\ C(s) = K (1 + \dfrac{1}{s T_i} + s T_d) \] with a measurement filter \(F(s)\) defined as \[ F(s) = \dfrac{1}{(s \theta)^2/2 + s \theta + 1} \]
