Van der Pol

rem: Van der Pol

The Van der Pol Oscillator evolves over time according to the second order differential equation:

\[{d^2x \over dt^2}-u(1-x^2){dx \over dt}+x=0\]

where \(x\) is the position coordinate, which is a function of the time \(t\), and \(u\) is a scalar parameter indicating the non-linearity and the strength of the damping. For \(u \gt \gt 1\) the oscillator is being damped, whereas for \(u \lt \lt 1\) energy is added to the system.
