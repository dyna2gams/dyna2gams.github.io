Parallel reaction in tubular reactor

rem: Parallel reaction in tubular reactor

This is an optimal control problem with the saturate control profile, where a tubular reactor makes two products according to the parallel reaction: \(A \rightarrow B, A \rightarrow C\). The objective is to maximize the yield of \(B\) at the final time. The dimensionless model describing the system is described here after with \(x_1\) as dimensionless concentration of \(A\) and \(x_2\) as dimensionless concentration of \(B\).

Reference:

  1. Xinggao Liu, Long Chen, Yunqing Hu, Solution of Chemical Dynamic Optimization Using the Simultaneous Strategies, Chinese Journal of Chemical Engineering, Volume 21, Issue 1, January 2013, Pages 55-63.

Optimal solution: \(J^* = 0.5738923\). 

par:
  tf = 1 

dyn:
  x1 x2
  u

lim:
  0 <= u <= 5
  
t=t0:
  x1 = 1
  x2 = 0

equ:
  x1´ == -(u+0.5*sqr(u))*x1
  x2´ == u*x1

obj:
  maximize final(x2) using nlp
